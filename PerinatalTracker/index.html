Aims

Our aim is to create a very focused app that will help with a facet of perinatal mental health treatment delivery. We are not looking for a “magic bullet” app. Our aim is to create an app that takes full advantage of a smartphone platform and will enable both the patient and the practitioner to make the treatment task a little bit easier.

Literature review

There is a large amount of literature on the use and effectiveness of smartphone apps in the monitoring and management of both general and perinatal mental health disorders. “Most studies in gestational diabetes and mental health show that eHealth applications are good alternatives to standard practice [ for example ] Web-based cognitive behavioral therapy.”Van Den Heuvel et al [8]

“Preliminary data suggests web-based therapies for perinatal depression delivered in the post-partum period may play a role in improving maternal mood but more studies are needed” Lee et al [4]

Authors stress that it is important to see these apps as an adjunct to rather than a replacement for traditional therapies “Technology should not be seen as a replacement for traditional psychotherapy, rather it offers new opportunities to support mental health as part of an overall ecosystem.”Stawarz et al [7]

Patient self monitoring is a function highlighted in many reviews. “Clients who record their own thoughts, feelings, and behaviors as part of a CBT-based intervention are able to reflect on their reports and exercise self-monitoring. Self-monitoring is a core feature of many evidence-based psychological therapeutic techniques, including CBT , mindfulness exercises , emotion-focused therapy , DBT, and acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT)“ Bakker et al[2]

How ever Stawarz et al [7] point out that there is a reluctance to use apps in clinical practice “that health professionals are less confident in the efficacy of apps, but it may also be because of institutional [..] lack of awareness of available apps.” They go on to say that to overcome this “further engagement with health care providers to understand their needs and raise awareness[.]”

The literature provides guidelines for the creation of efficacious apps for example Bakker et al[2] produced sixteen evidence based recommendations for MHapp design. But the most important is that they must be grounded in academic and experiential mental health knowledge.
